Personalized 7-Day Indian LCHF Meal Plan for Type 2 Diabetes

A seven-day, low-carb high-fat (LCHF) meal plan designed for a 40-year-old with Type 2 Diabetes and a BMI of 20.0, focusing on blood sugar management and weight maintenance. This plan uses only ingredients from the provided list, with the assumption that basic Indian spices (like turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala) and cooking oils derived from listed items (e.g., coconut oil) are permitted to meet the Indian cuisine preference. All meals are chicken-free.

Pre-Breakfast Appetizer: Lemon Water and a Few Almonds (07:30 AM)

Ingredients:
  • Water: 1 glass
  • Lemon juice, raw: 1 tbsp
  • Nuts, almonds, whole, raw: 15g (about 12 almonds)
Instructions:

Start your day with a glass of warm water with lemon juice. Have the almonds on the side.

Breakfast: Mushroom and Spinach Scrambled Eggs (08:30 AM)

Ingredients:
  • Egg, whole, raw, fresh: 3 large
  • Mushrooms, white, raw: 50g, sliced
  • Spinach, mature: 50g, raw
  • Coconut Oil: 1 tbsp
  • Onions, yellow, raw: 20g, chopped
  • Salt, Black Pepper, Turmeric Powder: to taste
Instructions:

Heat coconut oil in a pan. Sauté onions until translucent. Add mushrooms and cook until they release their water. Add spinach and cook until wilted. Whisk eggs with salt, pepper, and a pinch of turmeric. Pour into the pan and scramble until cooked to your liking.

Lunch: Spicy Lamb Curry with Cauliflower Rice (01:00 PM)

Ingredients:
  • Lamb, New Zealand, imported, square-cut shoulder...: 150g, cubed
  • Coconut Oil: 1 tbsp
  • Onions, yellow, raw: 50g, chopped
  • Garlic, raw: 2 cloves, minced
  • Ginger root, raw: 1 tsp, grated
  • Tomatoes, red, ripe, raw: 50g, chopped
  • Coconut Meat (for milk): 100ml full-fat coconut milk
  • Spices: Turmeric, Cumin, Coriander, Garam Masala, Chili Powder: to taste
  • Cauliflower, raw: 200g, grated for 'rice'
  • Coriander (cilantro) leaves, raw: for garnish
Instructions:

For curry: Heat oil, sauté onions, ginger, and garlic. Add spices and cook for a minute. Add lamb and brown on all sides. Add tomatoes and cook until soft. Add coconut milk and 1/2 cup water, simmer until lamb is tender (about 45-60 minutes). For rice: Steam or microwave grated cauliflower for 3-4 minutes until tender. Serve the curry over cauliflower rice, garnished with fresh coriander.

Snack: Avocado with Lime and Salt (04:30 PM)

Ingredients:
  • Avocados, raw, all commercial varieties: 100g (about half an avocado)
  • Lime juice, raw: 1 tsp
  • Salt: a pinch
Instructions:

Slice the avocado, drizzle with lime juice, and sprinkle with salt.

Dinner: Pan-Seared Tilapia with Sautéed Green Beans (07:30 PM)

Ingredients:
  • Fish, tilapia, raw: 150g fillet
  • Coconut Oil: 1.5 tbsp
  • Beans, snap, green, raw: 200g, trimmed
  • Garlic, raw: 2 cloves, sliced
  • Lemon juice, raw: 1 tbsp
  • Salt, Pepper, Chili Flakes: to taste
Instructions:

Pat the tilapia dry and season with salt and pepper. Heat 1 tbsp of oil in a pan and sear the fish for 3-4 minutes per side until cooked through. Set aside. In the same pan, add the remaining oil and sauté sliced garlic until fragrant. Add green beans and a splash of water, cover and cook for 5-7 minutes until tender-crisp. Drizzle with lemon juice and serve with the fish.

Lunch: Goat Kebabs with Mint-Coriander Chutney (01:00 PM)

Ingredients:
  • Game meat, goat, raw: 200g, minced
  • Onions, red, raw: 30g, finely chopped
  • Ginger root, raw: 1 tsp, grated
  • Garlic, raw: 1 clove, minced
  • Peppers, hot chili, green, raw: 1, finely chopped
  • Spices: Coriander Powder, Cumin, Garam Masala, Salt: to taste
  • Sesame Oil: 1 tbsp, for grilling/pan-frying
  • For Chutney: Coriander (cilantro) leaves, raw (50g), Lemon juice (2 tbsp), Green chili (1), Salt
  • Side Salad: Lettuce, leaf, green, raw (50g)
Instructions:

Combine minced goat, onion, ginger, garlic, chili, and spices. Form into long kebabs on skewers. Grill or pan-fry with sesame oil until cooked through. For chutney, blend all ingredients until smooth. Serve kebabs with the chutney and a side of lettuce.

Instructions:

A simple and nutritious snack.

Dinner: Smoky Eggplant Bharta with Pork (07:30 PM)

Ingredients:
  • Pork, fresh, loin, top loin (roasts), boneless...: 150g, cut into small pieces
  • Eggplant, raw: 200g (1 medium)
  • Coconut Oil: 1 tbsp
  • Onions, yellow, raw: 50g, chopped
  • Tomatoes, red, ripe, raw: 50g, chopped
  • Garlic, raw: 1 clove, minced
  • Spices: Turmeric, Cumin, Salt: to taste
Instructions:

Roast the eggplant directly over a flame or under a broiler until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft. Let it cool, then peel and mash the flesh. In a pan, heat oil and brown the pork pieces; set aside. In the same pan, sauté onions and garlic until golden. Add tomatoes and spices, cook until soft. Stir in the mashed eggplant and cooked pork. Mix well and cook for another 5 minutes.

Lunch: Shrimp Masala with Sautéed Cabbage (01:00 PM)

Ingredients:
  • Crustaceans, shrimp, raw: 200g, peeled and deveined
  • Coconut Oil: 1.5 tbsp
  • Onions, yellow, raw: 50g, sliced
  • Garlic, raw: 2 cloves, minced
  • Ginger root, raw: 1 tsp, grated
  • Tomatoes, red, ripe, raw: 100g, pureed
  • Spices: Turmeric, Chili Powder, Garam Masala, Salt: to taste
  • Cabbage, green, raw: 200g, shredded
  • Mustard seeds: 1/2 tsp
Instructions:

For shrimp: Heat 1 tbsp oil, sauté onions, ginger, and garlic. Add tomato puree and spices, cook until oil separates. Add shrimp and cook for 3-5 minutes until pink. For cabbage: Heat 1/2 tbsp oil, pop mustard seeds. Add cabbage, salt, and a pinch of turmeric. Sauté until tender. Serve the shrimp masala with the cabbage.

Snack: Macadamia Nuts (04:30 PM)

Ingredients:

Instructions:

Enjoy these buttery, low-carb nuts.

Dinner: Veal and Broccoli Stir-fry (07:30 PM)

Ingredients:
  • Veal, shoulder, arm, separable lean and fat, raw: 150g, thinly sliced
  • Broccoli, raw: 150g, florets
  • Sesame Oil: 1 tbsp
  • Coconut Aminos: 2 tbsp
  • Garlic, raw: 2 cloves, minced
  • Ginger root, raw: 1 tsp, grated
  • Peppers, bell, red, raw: 50g, sliced
  • Nuts, almonds, blanched: 30g
Instructions:

Marinate veal in coconut aminos, garlic, and ginger. Heat sesame oil in a wok or large pan. Add veal and stir-fry until browned. Add broccoli and bell peppers, stir-fry for 5-7 minutes until vegetables are tender-crisp. Toss in almonds before serving.

Lunch: Pork Vindaloo with Steamed Asparagus (01:00 PM)

Ingredients:
  • Pork, fresh, shoulder...: 150g, cubed
  • Coconut Oil: 1 tbsp
  • Onions, yellow, raw: 50g, chopped
  • Garlic, raw: 3 cloves
  • Ginger root, raw: 1 inch piece

Instructions:

Add 1 cup of water, bring to a boil, then simmer until pork is very tender. Steam asparagus until tender-crisp and serve on the side.

Dinner: Baked Salmon Tikka with Green Salad (07:30 PM)

Instructions:

Dinner: Stuffed Bitter Gourd (Karela) with Cod Fish (07:30 PM)

Ingredients:
  • Fish, cod, Atlantic, raw: 150g, flaked
  • Balsam-pear (bitter gourd), pods, raw: 150g (2 medium)
  • Onions, red, raw: 50g, finely chopped
  • Coconut Oil: 2 tbsp
  • Fennel Powder, Turmeric, Chili Powder, Salt: to taste
  • Lemon juice, raw: 1 tbsp
Instructions:

Slit bitter gourds, deseed, and rub with salt. Set aside for 30 mins, then wash. In a pan, heat 1 tbsp oil, sauté onions until translucent. Add flaked cod and spices, cook for 5 minutes. Stuff this mixture into the bitter gourds. In the same pan, add remaining oil and shallow-fry the stuffed gourds on low heat, covered, until tender. Squeeze lemon juice on top before serving.

Dinner: Spicy Goat Curry with Cauliflower Mash (07:30 PM)

Ingredients:
  • Game meat, goat, raw: 150g, bone-in pieces
  • Coconut Oil: 1.5 tbsp
  • Onions, yellow, raw: 50g, sliced
  • Ginger-Garlic paste: 1 tsp

  • Cauliflower, raw: 150g

Instructions:

For curry: In a pressure cooker, heat oil. Add onions and fry until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste and spices. Add goat meat and brown it. Add 1.5 cups of water, salt, and pressure cook until the meat is tender. For mash: Steam cauliflower until very soft. Mash with coconut cream, salt, and pepper. Serve the hot curry with the cauliflower mash.

Breakfast: Coconut Flour 'Dosa' with Tomato Chutney (08:30 AM)

Ingredients:
  • Coconut Flour: 30g
  • Egg, whole, raw, fresh: 1 large
  • Ground Flaxseed: 1 tbsp
  • Water: as needed for batter
  • Coconut Oil: 1 tbsp
  • For Chutney: Tomatoes (100g), Onion (20g), Garlic (1 clove), Chili (1/2), Salt
Instructions:

For dosa: Whisk coconut flour, egg, flaxseed, salt, and water to a thin batter. Let it rest for 5 mins. Heat oil on a non-stick pan and pour a ladle of batter, spreading it thin. Cook on both sides. For chutney: Sauté all chutney ingredients until soft, then blend into a paste. Serve dosa with chutney.

Dinner: Grilled Lamb Chops with Roasted Brussels Sprouts (07:30 PM)

Ingredients:
  • Lamb, New Zealand, imported, loin chop...: 200g (about 2 chops)
  • Brussels sprouts, raw: 100g, halved
  • Coconut Oil: 1.5 tbsp, melted
  • Garlic, raw: 2 cloves, minced
  • Rosemary, Salt, Pepper: to taste
Instructions:

Toss Brussels sprouts with 1 tbsp of coconut oil, salt, and pepper. Roast at 200°C (400°F) for 20-25 minutes until crispy. Rub lamb chops with remaining oil, minced garlic, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Grill or pan-sear for 3-5 minutes per side for medium-rare, or until desired doneness. Serve chops with the roasted sprouts.
