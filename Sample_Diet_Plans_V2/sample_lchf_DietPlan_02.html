Personalized 7-Day Indian LCHF Meal Plan for Type 2 Diabetes (Egg-Free)

Plan Overview

A seven-day, low-carb high-fat (LCHF) meal plan designed for a 40-year-old with Type 2 Diabetes and a BMI of 20.0, focusing on blood sugar management and weight maintenance. This plan is strictly egg-free and chicken-free, using only ingredients from the provided list. Recipes are adapted for an Indian cuisine preference, assuming the use of common Indian spices and cooking fats like ghee, which are included in the food list.

Daily Calorie Goal: 1750 kcal

Macronutrient Goals:
  • Net Carbohydrates: < 40g
  • Protein: 80-100g
  • Fat: 130-150g

Allergens & Restrictions Summary

Food Restrictions: Egg

Allergens: Nuts

Medical Conditions: Type 2 Diabetes

Important Note: This meal plan is for informational purposes only. Current evidence suggests that a low-carbohydrate diet can be safe and effective for people with diabetes. However, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare provider or a registered dietitian before making significant dietary changes, especially if you are on medication, to prevent adverse effects like hypoglycemia.

Lunch: Lamb Curry with Cauliflower Rice

Suggested Time: 01:00 PM

Ingredients:

  • Lamb, New Zealand, imported, square-cut shoulder, separable lean and fat, raw: 150g, cubed
  • Butter, Clarified butter (ghee): 1 tbsp
  • Onions, yellow, raw: 50g, chopped
  • Garlic, raw: 2 cloves, minced
  • Ginger root, raw: 1 tsp, grated
  • Tomatoes, red, ripe, raw: 50g, chopped
  • Coconut (for 100ml full-fat milk): 100ml
  • Spices: Turmeric, Cumin, Coriander, Garam Masala, Chili Powder: to taste
  • Cauliflower, raw: 200g, grated for 'rice'

Instructions:

For curry: Heat ghee in a pot, sauté onions, ginger, and garlic. Add spices and cook for a minute. Add lamb and brown on all sides. Add tomatoes and cook until soft. Add coconut milk and 1/2 cup water, simmer until lamb is tender. For rice: Steam or microwave grated cauliflower for 3-4 minutes. Serve curry over cauliflower rice.
