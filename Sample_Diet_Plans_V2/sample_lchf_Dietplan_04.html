Personalized 7-Day LCHF Indian Meal Plan (Non-Vegetarian, No Chicken)

A 40-year-old with Type 2 Diabetes, BMI 20.0.

Dietary Specifications

Allergens to Avoid Dairy, Soy
Other Food Restrictions No Chicken
Other Medical Conditions None specified

Important Notes

  • This meal plan is designed for blood sugar management and weight maintenance, providing approximately 1600-1800 kcal daily with very low net carbohydrates.
  • All recipes strictly use ingredients from the provided list. It is assumed that basic zero-carb Indian spices (turmeric, cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala, chili powder, salt), water, and common cooking methods (sautéing, grilling, steaming) are permitted to meet the 'Indian Cuisine' requirement.
  • Cooking fat used is primarily coconut oil, derived from 'Coconut' on the list, or animal fat rendered during cooking (e.g., from duck or pork).
  • Please monitor your blood glucose levels regularly. It is crucial to consult your doctor or a registered dietitian before starting any new dietary regimen, especially with Type 2 Diabetes.
  • Ensure adequate hydration by drinking plenty of water throughout the day.

Masala Scrambled Eggs (Anda Bhurji)

8:30 AM

Recipe:

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of coconut oil in a non-stick pan.
  2. Sauté 1/4 chopped yellow onion (25g) and 1 chopped green chili until the onion is translucent.
  3. Add 1/2 chopped roma tomato (30g) and cook for 2 minutes.
  4. Whisk 3 large fresh eggs and add them to the pan. Scramble gently.
  5. Season with salt and 1/4 teaspoon of turmeric powder. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
Nutrition: 355 kcal, Carbs: 8 g, Protein: 21 g, Fat: 27 g, Fiber: 2 g

Lamb Keema with Cauliflower

7:30 PM

Recipe:

LCHF Pork Vindaloo with Spinach

7:30 PM

Recipe:

  1. Cube 150g of pork shoulder. Marinate in a paste of 2 dried red chilies, 3 garlic cloves, 1/2 inch ginger, and 2 tbsp lime juice.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp coconut oil. Sauté 1/2 chopped onion (50g). Add the pork and marinade, and brown the meat.
  3. Add 1/2 cup of water, cover, and simmer for 45-60 minutes until the pork is tender and the gravy is thick.
  4. Serve with 100g of spinach, steamed or wilted on the side.
Nutrition: 912 kcal, Carbs: 6 g, Protein: 61 g, Fat: 65 g, Fiber: 1 g

Goat Curry with Almond Thickener

7:30 PM

Recipe:

  1. Heat 1.5 tbsp coconut oil. Sauté 1/2 chopped onion (50g), ginger-garlic paste, and 1/2 chopped tomato (30g).
  2. Add 150g of cubed goat meat and brown it. Add Indian spices (coriander powder, cumin, turmeric).

  4. In the last 10 minutes, stir in a paste made from 2 tbsp (20g) of ground raw almonds and a little water to thicken the curry.

Pan-Seared Duck Breast with Sautéed Broccoli

7:30 PM

Recipe:

  1. Score the skin of a 150g duck breast. Season with salt.
  2. Place skin-side down in a cold pan and turn heat to medium. Cook for 10-12 minutes, rendering the fat. Flip and cook for another 3-5 minutes.
  3. Remove duck to rest. Pour off most of the rendered fat, leaving 1 tbsp in the pan.
  4. Sauté 150g of broccoli florets in the duck fat until tender-crisp. Serve with the sliced duck breast.
Nutrition: 680 kcal, Carbs: 12 g, Protein: 51 g, Fat: 47 g, Fiber: 2 g

Garlic Shrimp with Amaranth Greens

7:30 PM

Recipe:
