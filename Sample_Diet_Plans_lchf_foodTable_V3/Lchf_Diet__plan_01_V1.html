7-Day LCHF Meal Plan

Restrictions: Dairy-Free, Soy-Free, No Chicken, Oil-Free

Plan Overview

Target Profile: A comprehensive, medically-safe, personalized LCHF seven-day meal plan for a 40-year-old person with Type 2 diabetes and a BMI of 20.0, aiming for weight maintenance and blood glucose stabilization. This plan is strictly dairy-free, soy-free, chicken-free, and oil-free, utilizing only the provided list of approved ingredients.

LCHF Approach: Therapeutic ketogenic diet for diabetes management, focusing on whole food fats, adequate protein, and minimal net carbohydrates (20-30g/day) to promote stable blood glucose, improve insulin sensitivity, and maintain a state of nutritional ketosis. All cooking is 100% oil-free.

Calorie Goal: Approximately 1800 kcal/day for weight maintenance.

Weekly Nutrition Summary

  • Average Daily Calories: 1805 kcal
  • Average Daily Net Carbs: 23.5g (5.2% of calories)
  • Average Daily Fat: 149.2g (74.4% of calories)
  • Average Daily Protein: 91.1g (20.2% of calories)

Day 1

Daily Summary: 1813 kcal | Net Carbs: 19.6g | Fat: 149.7g | Protein: 83.7g

Breakfast (8:00 AM) - Masala Avocado with Toasted Seeds

Preparation: Mash the avocado flesh in a bowl. Mix in lemon juice, salt, and a pinch of garam masala. In a dry pan over low heat, gently toast the flaxseeds and hemp seeds for 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Sprinkle the toasted seeds over the masala avocado.

Ingredients:
  • Avocado: 150g
  • Flaxseed: 20g
  • Hemp seeds: 20g
  • Lemon juice: 10ml

Lunch (1:00 PM) - Goan Mackerel Curry (Oil-free, Dairy-free)

Preparation: Create a smooth paste by blending raw coconut meat, garlic, ginger, turmeric, and chili powder with water. Water-sauté chopped onion until soft. Add the coconut paste and chopped tomato, and cook for 3-4 minutes. Add water and salt to form a gravy. Gently place mackerel pieces into the curry, cover, and simmer for 8-10 minutes until cooked. Garnish with coriander.

Ingredients:
  • Atlantic Mackerel: 150g
  • Raw Coconut Meat: 40g
  • Red Onion: 30g
  • Tomato: 50g
  • Garlic: 5g
  • Ginger: 5g
  • Coriander: 10g

Snack (4:00 PM) - Walnut and Macadamia Mix

Preparation: Combine raw walnuts and macadamia nuts in a small bowl.

Ingredients:
  • Walnuts: 25g
  • Macadamia Nuts: 25g

Dinner (7:30 PM) - Dry-Rubbed Lamb Chops with Mint-Avocado Mash

Preparation: Create a dry rub with salt, pepper, cumin, and coriander powder; rub it onto the lamb chops. Grill or broil the chops. Mash avocado with finely chopped mint, cilantro, and lemon juice. Serve the lamb chops with the avocado mash.

Ingredients:
  • Lamb Leg Chop/Steak: 150g
  • Avocado: 80g
  • Coriander: 10g

Day 2

Daily Summary: 1789 kcal | Net Carbs: 23.8g | Fat: 145.9g | Protein: 93.8g

Breakfast (8:00 AM) - Spicy Goat Mince (Kheema) with Cauliflower

Preparation: Water-sauté onion, ginger, and garlic. Add spices. Add the minced goat meat and brown it. Add chopped tomato and salt, cover, and cook until tender. In the last 5 minutes, add cauliflower florets and cook until tender. Garnish with cilantro.

Ingredients:
  • Goat Meat (minced): 150g
  • Cauliflower: 100g
  • Red Onion: 30g
  • Tomato: 50g

Lunch (1:00 PM) - Tandoori-Style Salmon with 'Raita'

Preparation: Marinate salmon fillet in lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, and tandoori spices. Broil or grill until cooked. For the dairy-free 'raita', mix diced cucumber into mashed avocado with chopped mint, cilantro, and salt. Serve salmon with raita and steamed broccoli.

Ingredients:
  • Atlantic Salmon: 180g
  • Avocado: 100g
  • Broccoli: 150g
  • Cucumber: 50g

Snack (4:00 PM) - Coconut and Almond Chunks

Preparation: A simple mix of raw fresh coconut meat chunks and whole raw almonds.

Ingredients:
  • Raw Coconut Meat: 50g
  • Almonds: 30g

Dinner (7:30 PM) - Okra (Bhindi) with Veal

Preparation: Water-sauté onion and ginger-garlic. Add spices. Add veal cubes and brown them. Add tomatoes and water, then simmer until veal is nearly tender. Add trimmed okra and cook until tender. Serve with a side of mashed avocado.

Ingredients:
  • Veal (cubed): 150g
  • Okra: 150g
  • Avocado: 50g
  • Onion & Tomato: 20g & 30g

Day 3

Daily Summary: 1821 kcal | Net Carbs: 24.3g | Fat: 151.8g | Protein: 89.9g

Breakfast (8:00 AM) - Coconut 'Upma' with Vegetables

Preparation: Grate fresh coconut and cauliflower. Water-sauté onion, green chili, and ginger. Add diced bell pepper and cauliflower 'rice' and sauté for 5 minutes. Stir in grated coconut, salt, and lemon juice. Garnish with cilantro.

Ingredients:
  • Raw Coconut Meat: 80g
  • Cauliflower: 150g
  • Red Onion: 30g
  • Green Bell Pepper: 50g

Lunch (1:00 PM) - Lamb Korma (Dairy-Free)

Preparation: Make a paste by blending soaked raw almonds with water, ginger, garlic, and spices. Grill or broil lamb cubes. Gently heat the almond paste with water to create a sauce. Add salt and the cooked lamb. Simmer gently for 10 minutes. Serve with steamed green beans.

Ingredients:
  • Lamb (cubed): 180g
  • Almonds: 50g
  • Green Beans: 100g

Snack (4:00 PM) - Avocado Boat with Hemp Seeds

Preparation: Cut an avocado in half, remove the pit. Sprinkle with salt and hulled hemp seeds.

Ingredients:
  • Avocado: 150g
  • Hemp Seeds: 20g

Dinner (7:30 PM) - Bengali Fish Jhol (Light Stew)

Preparation: Make a light spice paste. Simmer paste and salt in water. Add cod fillets and halved cherry tomatoes. Simmer for 7-8 minutes until fish is flaky. Garnish with cilantro. Serve with a side of mashed avocado to boost fat.

Ingredients:
  • Atlantic Cod: 200g
  • Avocado: 100g
  • Grape Tomatoes: 100g
  • Coriander: 20g

Day 4

Daily Summary: 1795 kcal | Net Carbs: 23.8g | Fat: 148.1g | Protein: 95.9g

Breakfast (8:00 AM) - 'Scrambled' Tofu-Style Dish (using Nuts)

Preparation: Soak macadamia nuts overnight. Blend with water, turmeric, and black salt to a coarse paste. Water-sauté onions and bell peppers. Add the nut 'scramble' and cook for 2-3 minutes. Fold in chopped spinach.

Ingredients:
  • Macadamia Nuts: 80g
  • Baby Spinach: 50g
  • Red Onion: 30g
  • Red Bell Pepper: 50g

Lunch (1:00 PM) - Palak Gosht (Spinach with Goat Meat)

Preparation: Pressure cook goat meat until tender. Water-sauté onion, add tomato and spices. Add blanched spinach puree. Add the cooked goat and broth and simmer for 10 minutes. Stir in ground flaxseed at the end to thicken.

Ingredients:
  • Goat Meat: 200g
  • Spinach: 200g
  • Flaxseed: 15g
  • Onion & Tomato: 30g & 50g

Snack (4:00 PM) - Pecan Halves

Preparation: A simple serving of raw pecan halves.

Ingredients:
  • Pecans: 30g

Dinner (7:30 PM) - Broiled Swordfish with Avocado Salsa

Preparation: Season swordfish steak and broil for 8-10 minutes. For the salsa, dice avocado and red onion, and mix with chopped cilantro and lime juice. Serve the swordfish topped with the salsa.

Ingredients:
  • Swordfish: 150g
  • Avocado: 100g
  • Red Onion: 20g
  • Coriander: 10g

Day 5

Preparation: Combine ground flaxseed, shredded raw coconut, and water in a pot. Simmer over low heat, stirring constantly until it thickens. Top with whole almonds.

Ingredients:

Ingredients:

LCHF Adaptation Tips

  • Embrace Water-Sautéing: Instead of oil, use small amounts of water or broth to sauté onions, garlic, and spices.
  • Whole Food Fats are Key: Your primary fats will come from avocado, nuts, seeds, coconut, and fatty fish.
  • Nut/Seed Creams: Create dairy-free cream sauces by blending soaked raw almonds, macadamia nuts, or coconut meat with water and spices.
  • Mind the Aromatics: Onions, garlic, and tomatoes contain carbohydrates that add up. Use them for flavor but be mindful of the quantities.

Diabetes & Ketosis Management

  • CRITICAL: Medical Supervision is Mandatory. Before starting this diet, consult with your endocrinologist. Your diabetes medications will likely need significant adjustment to prevent dangerous hypoglycemia (low blood sugar).
  • Monitor Blood Glucose Frequently: Check your blood sugar levels more often than usual, especially during the first few weeks.
  • Stay Hydrated: Ketogenic diets have a diuretic effect. Drink plenty of water throughout the day.
  • Manage Electrolytes: The initial water loss can deplete electrolytes. Ensure adequate intake of sodium, potassium, and magnesium through your food. Rich sources include avocados, spinach, salmon, and nuts.
